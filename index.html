Abstract

We are witnessing a paradigm shift in robot autonomy as Artificial Intelligence (AI) revolutionizes tasks traditionally handled by classical models of perception, planning, and control. In resource-constrained, high-speed, and harsh environments, AI-driven methods are increasingly favored due to the challenges in obtaining precise mathematical models. These techniques offer superior performance, improved policy optimization, better handling of edge cases, and robustness to unpredictable disturbances.

To push the boundaries of AI in real-time, resource-constrained settings, we need a systematic approach for data generation, efficient neural network architectures, and minimal world models for robust deployment on aerial robots. These advancements will enable autonomous aerial vehicles to operate in real-world conditions without relying on external infrastructure.

This workshop aims to highlight these critical developments and foster discussions on how they can facilitate the widespread deployment of autonomous aerial robots for humanitarian applications. From plant pollination to search and rescue, and combating poaching, the potential impact is vast. We hope this workshop will inspire greater attention and collaboration within the robotics community, accelerating the adoption of embodied AI in aerial robotics for the betterment of society.
